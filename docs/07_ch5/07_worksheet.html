Star Trek: The Next Generation Dialogue

The data in trek.csv contain word counts and proportions of words spoken by the main characters from Star Trek: The Next Generation. The original dataset was obtained from github.com/RMHogervorst/TNG. Each row summarizes the dialogue for one character in one season. The variables are:

  • season: the season of the show (1–7).
  • character: the character’s name (lower case).
  • count: total word count for that character in that season.
  • prop: proportion of all words in that season spoken by that character.
  • logprop: natural log of prop.

Captain Picard is generally considered the main character. We want to answer three questions:

  1. Do characters have about the same amount of dialogue?
  2. Does Picard speak more than the other characters?
  3. Do the other characters speak about the same amount as each other?

Note: ANOVA is not quite the ideal method here because the independence assumption is somewhat violated — as one character speaks more, the others must speak less within each season. We will proceed anyway and treat any conclusions as preliminary.

We will use logprop as our response throughout.
