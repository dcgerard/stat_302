Case Study 3.1.2: Effects of Agent Orange on Troops in Vietnam

Many Vietnam veterans are concerned that their health may have been affected by exposure to Agent Orange, a herbicide sprayed in South Vietnam between 1962 and 1970. The particularly worrisome component of Agent Orange is a dioxin called TCDD, which in high doses is known to be associated with certain cancers. Studies have shown that high levels of this dioxin can be detected 20 or more years after heavy exposure. Consequently, researchers from the Centers for Disease Control compared dioxin levels in Vietnam veterans to those in veterans who did not serve in Vietnam.

The 646 Vietnam veterans in the study were a sample of U.S. Army combat personnel who served in Vietnam during 1967 and 1968, in areas most heavily treated with Agent Orange. The 97 non-Vietnam veterans entered the Army between 1965 and 1971 and served only in the United States or Germany. Neither sample was randomly selected.

Blood samples from each veteran were analyzed for the presence of dioxin.
